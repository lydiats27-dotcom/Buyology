## Abstract

**SmartQSR AI** is an AI-powered real-time order verification system designed to improve order accuracy in Quick Service Restaurants (QSRs). The system combines edge computing and computer vision to automatically verify food items before they are handed to customers, reducing human error, food wastage, and operational costs.

The solution uses a **Raspberry Pi** connected to a **USB webcam** to capture multiple images of prepared food trays. A custom-trained **YOLOv8** object detection model identifies food items such as burgers, fries, beverages, wraps, nuggets, pizzas, and other menu items. The detected items are then compared with the expected order generated by the restaurant's Point of Sale (POS) system through a **FastAPI** backend. If the detected items match the customer's order, the system activates a green LED indicating successful verification. If discrepancies such as missing or incorrect items are detected, a red LED and buzzer immediately alert restaurant staff before the order is dispatched.

The application includes a modern **React-based dashboard** that allows restaurant staff to generate orders, monitor verification results, and maintain logs of all verification activities for future analysis. The AI model was trained on a custom dataset containing over **4,100 labeled images** across **10 food categories**, achieving an overall **Precision of 90%**, **Recall of 86%**, and **mAP@50 of 88.6%**, enabling reliable real-time detection suitable for deployment on low-cost edge devices.

SmartQSR AI demonstrates how artificial intelligence can automate quality assurance in restaurants, enhance customer satisfaction, minimize costly packing errors, and provide a scalable, affordable solution for restaurants, cloud kitchens, cafeterias, and food service businesses seeking smarter and more efficient operations.

## 1. Introduction


**SmartQSR AI** is an AI-powered real-time order verification system designed to improve order accuracy in Quick Service Restaurants (QSRs). By combining **Computer Vision**, **Edge Computing**, and **Artificial Intelligence**, the system automatically verifies prepared food orders before they are handed to customers, helping restaurants reduce packing errors, food wastage, refunds, and customer complaints.

A custom-trained **YOLOv8** object detection model identifies food items in real time and compares them against the customer's expected order received from the Point of Sale (POS) system through a **FastAPI** backend. SmartQSR AI also features a **React-based dashboard** for order generation, verification monitoring, and logging. Trained on a custom dataset of **4,100+ labeled images** across **10 food categories**, the system delivers fast and accurate object detection while remaining lightweight enough to run on edge devices.

**SmartQSR AI** aims to make restaurant operations smarter by delivering accurate, efficient, and scalable order verification through artificial intelligence.

## 2. Literature Review

The increasing adoption of Artificial Intelligence and Computer Vision has transformed automation across industries, including the food service sector. Recent research highlights the effectiveness of deep learning-based object detection models for real-time recognition tasks, making them suitable for applications such as food identification, inventory monitoring, and quality assurance.

The **YOLO (You Only Look Once)** family of object detection models has emerged as one of the most efficient approaches for real-time object detection due to its high speed and accuracy. In particular, **YOLOv8** offers improved detection performance while remaining lightweight enough for deployment on edge devices such as the Raspberry Pi. Several studies have demonstrated its effectiveness in detecting multiple food items under varying lighting conditions and camera angles.

Edge computing has also gained significant attention for enabling AI inference directly on embedded devices without relying on cloud infrastructure. Running inference locally reduces network latency, improves response time, enhances data privacy, and allows real-time decision-making—making it an ideal choice for restaurant automation systems.

Existing restaurant automation solutions primarily focus on billing, inventory management, or customer ordering. However, very few systems address **real-time order verification before food is dispatched**. Most restaurants continue to rely on manual verification, which is prone to human error, especially during peak business hours.

SmartQSR AI builds upon these research advancements by integrating **YOLOv8**, **OpenCV**, **FastAPI**, and **Raspberry Pi-based edge computing** into a single intelligent order verification system. By automatically comparing detected food items with the customer's expected order, the system helps reduce packing errors, improve operational efficiency, minimize food wastage, and enhance overall customer satisfaction.

The project demonstrates how modern AI technologies can be effectively deployed in Quick Service Restaurants (QSRs) to create a scalable, affordable, and real-time quality assurance solution.

## 3. Problem Statement


Quick Service Restaurants (QSRs) operate in fast-paced environments where speed and efficiency are critical. During peak hours, staff must prepare and package hundreds of customer orders within a short period, making manual verification both challenging and unreliable. As a result, incorrect or incomplete orders are common, leading to customer dissatisfaction, increased refunds, food wastage, and additional operational costs.

Current verification processes rely heavily on human intervention, which is prone to errors caused by fatigue, distractions, and high workloads. Existing restaurant management systems primarily focus on billing, order processing, and inventory management but do not provide an automated mechanism to verify whether the correct food items have been packed before dispatch.

There is a growing need for an intelligent, affordable, and real-time order verification system that can automatically compare prepared food items with the customer's order before it leaves the restaurant.

**SmartQSR AI** addresses this challenge by leveraging Artificial Intelligence, Computer Vision, and Edge Computing to detect food items in real time using a Raspberry Pi and a custom-trained YOLOv8 model. The system compares the detected items with the expected order, instantly alerts staff to any discrepancies through LED indicators and a buzzer, and helps ensure that every order is accurate before delivery, thereby improving operational efficiency and enhancing customer satisfaction.

## 5. Methodology

The SmartQSR AI system follows a structured methodology that combines **Artificial Intelligence**, **Computer Vision**, and **Edge Computing** to perform real-time order verification in Quick Service Restaurants (QSRs).

## 5.1 Data Collection & Annotation

A custom dataset containing over **4,100 images** of food items was collected and annotated using **Roboflow**. The dataset includes 10 food categories such as burgers, fries, pizza, wraps, nuggets, beverages, and garlic bread. The annotated dataset was divided into training, validation, and testing sets.

## 5.2 AI Model Training

The annotated dataset was used to train a **YOLOv8 (You Only Look Once Version 8)** object detection model. The model learns to accurately identify multiple food items in real time and generates a trained **best.pt** model for deployment.

## 5.3 Edge Device Deployment

The trained YOLOv8 model was deployed on a **Raspberry Pi** connected to a USB webcam. The camera captures multiple frames of the prepared food tray, and the Raspberry Pi performs on-device inference without requiring cloud processing.

## 5.4 Real-Time Food Detection

The webcam captures images of the packed order. The YOLOv8 model detects all visible food items and identifies their class labels and quantities. Multiple frames are processed to improve detection reliability and reduce false detections.

## 5.5 Order Verification

The restaurant's POS system sends the expected customer order to a **FastAPI** backend. The detected food items are compared with the expected order. The system checks for:

* Missing items
* Extra items
* Quantity mismatches

## 5.6 Alert & Feedback System

Based on the verification results:

* **Green LED** indicates the order is correct.
* **Red LED** and **buzzer** alert staff when incorrect or missing items are detected.

This enables immediate correction before the order reaches the customer.

## 5.7 Dashboard & Logging

A **React-based dashboard** allows restaurant staff to generate orders, monitor verification status, and view verification logs. The system records expected items, detected items, verification status, and timestamps for future analysis and quality control.

## Overall Workflow

Customer Order → POS System → Camera Capture → YOLOv8 Detection → FastAPI Verification → LED/Buzzer Feedback → Verification Logs
